# Accessibility Review Checklist

## Structure

- Page has meaningful title, `h1`, landmarks, and heading order.
- Buttons are buttons and navigation links are links.
- Custom controls have complete keyboard behavior.
- Icon-only controls have accessible names.

## Keyboard And Focus

- Primary flow works with keyboard only.
- Focus is visible on all interactive controls.
- Focus order matches visual order.
- Modal, drawer, dropdown, tab, and accordion focus behavior is defined.
- Focus is restored after modal or drawer close.

## Forms

- Every input has a visible label.
- Helper and error text are associated with fields.
- Validation errors are specific and recoverable.
- Required fields are communicated in text.
- Submission states are accessible.

## Tables And Data

- Data tables have headers and clear row context.
- Sorting, filtering, selection, pagination, and bulk actions are accessible.
- Charts have text summaries or data alternatives.
- Empty, loading, error, and permission states are accessible.

## Visual And Motion

- Text and UI contrast are acceptable.
- UI works at 200% zoom.
- Touch targets are comfortable on mobile.
- Meaning does not rely on color alone.
- Reduced-motion preference is respected.

## Content

- Action labels describe outcomes.
- Error and empty-state copy explains recovery.
- Images have correct alt policy.
- Dates, numbers, currency, and units are unambiguous.
